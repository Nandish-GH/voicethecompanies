    Welcome to Social Media Fundamentals

    INTRO A Note Before You Begin

    Welcome back. Now that your website basics are in place, let’s get your social profiles working for you too. Whether you are starting from scratch or dusting off old accounts, this lesson keeps it simple and step-by-step. You do not need to be a marketing expert. You just need a clear plan and a consistent routine.

    Social media can feel like a lot, but it gets much easier once you focus on the basics. We will help you pick the right platforms, set up a solid profile, and post consistently without overthinking it.

    Background

    Why Social Media Matters for Your Business

    CONTEXT The Big Picture

    Before we get into the steps, it helps to understand why social media is worth your time. These numbers come from across the industry and reflect how today's customers actually make decisions about where to spend their money.

    5B+
    People use social media worldwide as of 2024
    76%
    Of consumers have purchased something after seeing it on social media
    88%
    Of people who search Google for a local business visit or call within 24 hours
    3×
    More engagement on business pages that post consistently vs. sporadically

    Your potential customers are already on these platforms every day. When they need a product or service you offer, they search - and businesses with active, complete profiles consistently get chosen over those without them. Setting up these accounts is not just a marketing exercise: it is table stakes for being found.

    📸
    Instagram
    Visual Discovery

    Best for showing your products, behind-the-scenes moments, and building a visual identity. Ideal if your business has a strong visual component - food, fashion, retail, beauty, and more.

    👥
    Facebook
    Community & Reach

    The largest platform by users. Ideal for sharing updates, running events, collecting reviews, and reaching a broad local audience. Facebook Business Pages are also linked to Instagram ads.

    📍
    Google Business
    Local Search

    Not just social media - but essential. When someone searches "near me," your Google Business profile is what they see first. It drives directions, calls, and walk-in traffic more than any other platform.

    A word of encouragement: You do not need thousands of followers to see results from social media. Consistent, authentic posts to a small, engaged local audience will outperform sporadic posts to a large but disengaged one every time. This lesson will show you how.
    Step 1 of 7

    Search & Claim Your Profiles

    STEP 01 Your business may already exist online - find out now

    This is one of the most important things in this entire lesson, and most business owners skip it entirely. Before you create anything new, search for your business on each platform. Customers, former employees, or the platforms themselves may have already created a listing for you - and you may not even know it exists.

    📸
    Searching on Instagram
    Look for your business name in the search bar
    How to Search

    Open Instagram and tap the magnifying glass icon. Type your business name and look through the results. Check both Accounts and Places tabs. If you find a listing you didn't create, note the username.

    If You Find an Unofficial Account

    If another account is using your business name without your permission, tap the three dots on the profile and select "Report." Choose "It's pretending to be me or my business." Instagram's support team will review the claim.

    👥
    Searching on Facebook
    A Page may have been auto-created for your business
    How to Search

    Go to Facebook.com and type your business name in the search bar. Filter results to show only Pages. If a page for your business exists but you didn't create it, it may be an "Unofficial Page" that Facebook auto-generated from check-ins.

    Claiming an Unofficial Facebook Page

    Scroll to the bottom of the unofficial page and look for the option "Is this your business?" or "Claim this Page." You will be asked to verify ownership through your business email, phone number, or official documents. Once verified, you gain full control.

    📍
    Searching on Google Business
    This one is especially important - Google creates listings automatically
    How to Search

    Go to Google.com and type your business name and city. Look at the right-hand side of the results - a Google Business card may already appear. If you see one, look for "Own this business?" or "Claim this business" at the bottom.

    Why This Matters So Much

    Google auto-creates Business profiles from public data sources. These unclaimed profiles may have incorrect hours, phone numbers, or even outdated photos. Claiming yours gives you control and dramatically improves how your business appears in local search results.

    Our recommendation: Complete this search step right now, before moving on. Open Google, Instagram, and Facebook in three separate tabs and search your business name on each one. It takes five minutes and can save you from a lot of confusion later.
    Step 2 of 7

    Set Up a Business Account

    STEP 02 Always use a Business Account, not a personal one

    There is an important difference between a personal account and a Business Account on each platform. Business Accounts come with features that personal accounts do not - including analytics, contact buttons, ad tools, and the ability to schedule posts. If you already have a personal account for your business, this step explains how to upgrade it.

    Platform Account Type Key Business Features How to Set Up
    Instagram Instagram Business Account Insights dashboard, contact button, category label, ad access, scheduling via Meta Business Suite Go to Settings → Account → Switch to Professional Account → Select Business
    Facebook Facebook Page (not Profile) Page Insights, reviews, events, appointment booking, linked to Instagram ads facebook.com/pages/create - choose Business or Brand
    Google Google Business Profile Appears in Maps and Search, shows hours/reviews/photos, Q&A section, free for all businesses business.google.com - Sign in with a Google account and follow the setup wizard
    ⚠ Do Not Use a Personal Account for Your Business

    Personal Instagram profiles and personal Facebook profiles are designed for individuals, not businesses. They lack the tools you need, they can look unprofessional to customers, and they may be restricted from using advertising features. If you've been using a personal profile for your business, switch it to a Business Account right away - you will not lose your existing posts or followers.

    Good news: Switching to a Business Account on Instagram or Facebook is free and completely reversible. Your content, followers, and history stay exactly the same.
    Step 3 of 7

    Write Your Bio

    STEP 03 Your bio is your digital handshake - make it count

    Your bio is one of the very first things a potential customer reads about you. On Instagram, you have 150 characters. On Facebook, slightly more. On Google, you have a short description field. Every word matters. A great bio answers three questions immediately: Who are you? What do you do? How do I reach you?

    ❌   Before - Weak Bio
    "Welcome to our page! We sell products and services for all your needs. Follow us for updates!"

    What's wrong: Vague, no location, no specifics, no call to action. A customer reading this has no idea what you actually do or where you are.
    ✓   After - Strong Bio
    "Family-owned Caribbean bakery in Newark, NJ 🍞 Custom cakes, fresh bread, daily specials. Order online or call us!"

    Why it works: Specific business type, location, what you sell, how to take action. A reader knows immediately if this is the right place for them.
    TEMPLATE Fill-In-the-Blank Bio Formula

    Use this template to write your bio. Keep the total under 150 characters for Instagram.

    Your Bio Template [Type of Business] in [Your City, State]
    [What you offer - 2 or 3 specific things]
    [Call to action: Order online / Call us / Visit us / DM to book]

    Example: "Afro-Caribbean hair salon in Trenton, NJ · Natural styles, braids & locs · Book your appointment below 💚"

    Pro tip: Use the exact same bio (or a very similar one) across all three platforms. Consistency helps customers recognize your brand immediately, regardless of which platform they find you on first.
    TIPS Bio Best Practices
    • Be specific about your location. "Newark, NJ" is better than just "New Jersey." Local search works better with specific city names.
    • Include your strongest offering. Don't list everything you do - pick your top two or three products or services.
    • End with a call to action. Tell people what to do next: "Order below," "DM to book," "Link in bio," or "Call us."
    • Use an emoji or two sparingly. A single relevant emoji can make your bio easier to scan, but don't overdo it.
    • Avoid jargon. Write for a customer who has never heard of you before, not for someone who already knows your industry.
    Step 4 of 7

    Add a Profile Photo

    STEP 04 A strong profile photo builds immediate trust

    Your profile photo appears everywhere your business does - in search results, in comments, in messages, and at the top of your page. It should be professional, recognizable, and consistent across all platforms. Here is what works best for each situation.

    If You Have… Use This as Your Photo Tips
    A logo Your logo on a white or solid background Make sure it is readable at small sizes. Avoid thin text. Use a PNG file for crisp edges.
    A storefront or physical location A clean, well-lit exterior or interior photo Take it on a bright day. Remove clutter from the frame. Crop tightly to show your signage.
    A service or solo business A clear, friendly headshot of yourself Good lighting, neutral background, and a genuine smile. You are your brand - let people see you.
    No photo yet A simple text-based placeholder with your business initials Something is better than a blank default icon. Ask your VTC team to help you create a quick branded placeholder.
    Important: Use the exact same profile photo on Instagram, Facebook, and Google Business. This consistency is what makes your brand feel professional and trustworthy even before a customer reads a single word.
    SPECS Photo Technical Requirements
    • File type: JPG or PNG. Use PNG for logos to preserve sharp edges.
    • Recommended size: At least 400 × 400 pixels. Instagram displays it as a circle, so center your subject.
    • File size: Under 5 MB. Larger files will be compressed by the platforms anyway.
    • Avoid: Blurry, dark, or heavily filtered photos. Customers should be able to immediately recognize what your business is.
    Step 5 of 7

    Link All Platforms Together

    STEP 05 Connect your digital presence into one cohesive ecosystem

    Having three separate accounts is helpful. Having three accounts that all point to each other - and to your website - is significantly more powerful. When platforms are linked, a customer who finds you anywhere can immediately get to the rest of your presence. This also helps search engines understand that all of these profiles belong to the same business.

    A
    Link Instagram to Facebook
    These two platforms work best as a connected pair

    Go to your Instagram Business Account settings and select "Linked Accounts." Connect your Facebook Page from there. Once linked, you can share posts to both platforms simultaneously and manage both from Meta Business Suite (a free tool we'll cover in Step 7).

    B
    Add Your Website URL to All Profiles
    Always give people a direct path to your website

    Every platform has a field for your website URL. Fill it in everywhere: Instagram bio link, Facebook Page "About" section, and Google Business Profile "Website" field. This is where customers go when they are ready to take action - do not make them search for it.

    C
    Add Social Links to Your Website
    Two-way connections strengthen your digital presence

    Once your accounts are set up, ask your Voice the Companies student team to add your Instagram and Facebook icons to your website footer or header. This creates a two-way connection: social media → website, and website → social media. This mutual linking also improves how your business appears in Google searches.

    D
    Make Sure Your Contact Info Matches Everywhere
    Inconsistent info confuses both customers and search engines

    Your phone number, address, and business hours should be identical across your website, Instagram, Facebook, and Google Business. If one platform shows Monday–Saturday 9am–6pm and another shows Monday–Friday 10am–5pm, customers will not know which one to trust - and many will simply choose a competitor instead.

    Step 6 of 7

    Build a Simple Content Calendar

    STEP 06 Start with just 3 posts per week - consistency beats volume every time

    The biggest mistake small business owners make on social media is trying to post every day for two weeks and then burning out and going silent for a month. Sporadic posting tells the algorithm - and your customers - that you are unreliable. Three posts a week, every week, is dramatically more effective than an inconsistent burst of daily posts.

    Think of your social media like a conversation, not a broadcast. You wouldn't start a conversation with someone, talk nonstop for two weeks, and then disappear for a month. Show up consistently, and your audience will grow steadily and reliably.

    IDEAS 12 Content Ideas to Fill Your Calendar

    Rotate through these ideas week to week. You never need to come up with something brand new from scratch.

    • Introduce yourself or your story. People buy from people they trust. Share why you started your business.
    • Feature a product or service. One thing at a time. Clear photo, short description, and price if relevant.
    • Share a customer review or testimonial. Word-of-mouth, amplified. Screenshot a review or write it out as a graphic.
    • Behind the scenes. Show your process. A quick photo or video of how you make, prepare, or deliver what you offer.
    • Answer a common question. Educational content builds authority. What do customers ask you all the time?
    • Share a tip or piece of advice related to your industry. Generous, helpful content attracts loyal followers.
    • Announce a special, sale, or event. Give people a reason to act now.
    • Repost a community event or partner business. Community goodwill is real and reciprocated.
    • Show a before and after. Transformation stories are among the most-shared types of content on social media.
    • Celebrate a milestone. One year in business, 100 customers, first big order - share it.
    • Ask your audience a question. Engagement goes up when you invite people to respond.
    • Seasonal or holiday content. Tie your posts to events your customers are already thinking about.

    Step 7 of 7

    Schedule Posts in Advance

    STEP 07 Use free tools so you're never scrambling to post at the last minute

    Posting in real time is stressful. On a busy Monday morning, the last thing you want to think about is what to put on Instagram. Scheduling tools let you prepare your posts for the entire week on Sunday afternoon - and then forget about it. The posts go out automatically while you focus on running your business.

    🛠
    Meta Business Suite (Free)
    business.facebook.com - manages both Facebook and Instagram together
    What It Does

    Meta Business Suite is a free tool from Meta (the company behind Facebook and Instagram) that lets you manage both platforms from one place. You can create posts, schedule them for future dates and times, respond to messages and comments, and view analytics - all without switching back and forth between apps.

    How to Access It

    Go to business.facebook.com and sign in with your Facebook account. Your connected Instagram Business Account will also appear there. From the left menu, select "Planner" or "Create Post" and toggle on the scheduling option before you publish.

    Our Recommendation

    Spend 30–60 minutes every Sunday preparing and scheduling your three posts for the coming week. Set them for Monday, Wednesday, and Friday mornings between 9–11am - this is typically when engagement is highest for most small businesses.

    🛠
    Google Business - Post from the App
    The Google Business app lets you post updates directly to your listing
    Google Posts

    Google Business Profiles support a feature called "Posts" - short updates that appear directly in your search listing when someone finds you on Google. Use them to announce specials, share new products, or post events. They disappear after 7 days, so aim to add a new one each week.

    How to Create a Google Post

    Download the "Google Business Profile" app on your phone, sign in with the account linked to your profile, and tap "Create Post." You can add a photo, a short description, and a button (like "Order Now" or "Learn More"). It takes less than two minutes.

    ⚠ A Common Mistake to Avoid

    Do not connect every platform to a single auto-posting tool and share the exact same post everywhere at the exact same time with the exact same caption. Each platform has a different audience and a different format. A great Instagram caption with hashtags looks out of place on Google Business. Adapt your content slightly for each platform, even if the photo is the same.

    Simple rule: Same photo, slightly different caption. Instagram gets hashtags. Facebook gets a full sentence or two. Google Business gets a short announcement-style description.
    Summary

    Key Takeaways

    SUMMARY The Four Things That Matter Most
    01
    Consistency matters more than volume.

    Three posts a week, every week, will outperform ten posts in one week followed by silence. The algorithm rewards regularity, and so do your customers. Pick a schedule you can realistically maintain and stick to it.

    02
    Your bio is your first impression - make it specific and clear.

    A vague bio loses potential customers in seconds. Use the bio formula: who you are, where you are, what you offer, and what to do next. Keep it under 150 characters on Instagram, and use the same language everywhere.

    03
    Google Business is often overlooked but drives significant local foot traffic.

    Most small business owners focus on Instagram and forget that Google is where customers go right before they make a decision. A complete, active Google Business Profile with photos and weekly posts can be one of the most powerful things you do for local visibility.

    04
    You don't need to be on every platform. Start with two.

    Spreading yourself across six platforms with low-quality, inconsistent content is worse than being excellent on two. Choose Instagram and Facebook to start - or Instagram and Google Business if your business is very location-focused. Add platforms only when you feel confident with the first two.
